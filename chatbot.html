I want to apply for child custody. What's the process?
Just now

Child custody applications involve several steps:

  1. File a petition: Submit a custody petition to the Family Court in your district
  2. Required documents: Birth certificate, marriage/divorce papers, income proof, address proof
  3. Court process: Attend hearings where the court evaluates what's best for the child
  4. Types of custody: You can apply for physical custody, legal custody, or both

The court primarily considers the child's welfare in its decision. Is this part of a divorce proceeding or a separate application?

Just now
How do I file an FIR? What information is needed?
Just now

Filing an FIR (First Information Report) is the first step to report a cognizable offense. Here's the process:

  1. Visit the police station: Go to the police station with jurisdiction where the crime occurred
  2. Provide details: Include the following information:
    • Your personal details (name, address, contact information)
    • Date, time, and location of the incident
    • Detailed description of what happened
    • Names and descriptions of suspects (if known)
    • Names of witnesses (if any)
    • Details of any evidence or stolen property
  3. Review and sign: Read the written FIR before signing it
  4. Get a copy: You're entitled to a free copy of the FIR

If the police refuse to file your FIR, you can approach the Superintendent of Police or file a complaint directly to the magistrate under Section 156(3) CrPC.

Is NyayaBot's legal advice legally binding?

No, NyayaBot provides general legal information and guidance but not formal legal advice. It's designed to help you understand legal concepts and procedures, but for specific cases, we recommend consulting a licensed attorney who can provide legally binding advice tailored to your situation.

How accurate is the information provided?

NyayaBot is trained on comprehensive legal databases that are regularly updated to reflect current laws. While we strive for accuracy, laws and regulations can change. Always verify critical information with official sources or legal professionals, especially for time-sensitive matters.

Is my conversation with NyayaBot confidential?

Yes, all conversations with NyayaBot are end-to-end encrypted and confidential. We do not share your specific queries or personal information with third parties. However, anonymized data may be used to improve the system's responses and accuracy over time.

Can I download a transcript of my conversation?

Yes, you can download or email a transcript of your conversation with NyayaBot. At the end of your chat session, click on the "Save Conversation" button in the toolbar. You can choose to receive it as a PDF document or have it emailed to you for future reference.

What languages does NyayaBot support?

NyayaBot currently supports Hindi, English, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, Marathi, Gujarati, and Kannada. We're continuously working to add more Indian languages to make legal assistance accessible to more people across the country.

How does NyayaBot stay updated with legal changes?

Our team of legal experts and engineers regularly updates NyayaBot's knowledge base with the latest legal changes, amendments, and precedents. The system is refreshed with new legal information monthly, and critical updates are implemented immediately when significant legal changes occur.

Can NyayaBot help with document preparation?

Yes, NyayaBot can provide templates and guidance for many common legal documents such as rental agreements, employment contracts, and legal notices. However, for complex documents like wills, trusts, or specialized contracts, we recommend professional legal assistance for final review.

Is there a limit to how many questions I can ask?

No, there is no limit to the number of questions you can ask NyayaBot. Our service is designed to be accessible 24/7, and you can ask as many follow-up questions as you need to fully understand your legal situation. The service is completely free to use.
